CO-OWNERSHIP AND PROPERTY OPERATING AGREEMENT

Draft Version 0.4 — Subject to Review and Mutual Revision

THIS CO-OWNERSHIP AGREEMENT (the "Agreement") is drafted on this 31st day of May, 2026, to establish a collaborative home co-ownership framework between:

PARTY A: Jared Carter, Melinda Carter, and Nelson Carter (collectively the "Carter Partners") residing in Utah, and
PARTY B: ________________________ (the "Co-Owner Partners") residing in Utah.

The parties are referred to individually as a "Co-owner" and collectively as the "Co-owners" or "Partners".

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Co-owners intend to purchase a single-family residential property in Davis County, Utah (the "Property") to build equity and achieve their personal housing goals;

WHEREAS, the Co-owners wish to define their respective rights and responsibilities regarding ownership, financing, property management, private living arrangements, and the eventual refinancing or buyout of the Property;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein and other valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Co-owners agree as follows:

ARTICLE I: TITLE DEED, DOWN PAYMENT & EQUITY SHARES

Section 1.1 - Property Acquisition and Title. The Co-owners shall acquire the Property as Tenants in Common (TIC). The deed will record the specific fractional ownership interest of each Co-owner. Unless otherwise specified in writing, these fractional interests shall dictate each partner's share of capital contributions (including down payment), monthly mortgage payments, property insurance, and property taxes.

Section 1.2 - Ownership Shares. The ownership shares for the Property will be established at closing. The two standard options under consideration are:

Option Jared, Melinda & Nelson Carter Share Co-Owner Partners Share Down Payment Proportions
Option 1 (Equal) 50% Ownership Share 50% Ownership Share 50% / 50% down payment splits
Option 2 (Weighted) 60% Ownership Share 40% Ownership Share 60% / 40% down payment splits

Section 1.3 - Capital Account Bookkeeping. The Financial Administrator shall maintain a transparent capital ledger. Each Co-owner’s Capital Account will record:

  1. Their initial down payment contribution;
  2. Any additional funds contributed for capital improvements (such as roof, HVAC, or structural updates) approved by both Co-owners;
  3. The proportional share of mortgage principal paid off by each partner during the term of the mortgage.

No party shall withdraw capital from the Property ledger or borrow against the equity of the Property without the express, unanimous written consent of both Co-owners.

ARTICLE II: LOGISTICS, ROLES & DUAL-LIVING BOUNDARIES

Section 2.1 - Private Living Areas and Spatial Boundaries. The Property must feature two fully self-contained living areas, typically consisting of a Main House and a private finished basement or Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU). To ensure mutual privacy:

  • Each unit shall have its own independent exterior entrance with unique secure locks. Any connecting doors between the units must remain locked at all times.
  • Each unit must include its own separate amenities, including a kitchen, laundry facilities, bedrooms, bathrooms, and living areas.

Section 2.2 - Property Roles and Responsibilities. To ensure the property is managed efficiently and expectations remain clear, operational duties will be split into two primary roles:

Role 1: The Financial Administrator (Carter Partners). Responsible for managing joint accounts, executing monthly mortgage and escrow payments, maintaining utility and tax records, calculating monthly rent equalization adjustments, and providing a transparent monthly statement to both parties.

Role 2: The Property Manager (Co-Owner Partners). Responsible for coordinating routine physical maintenance, managing lawn care and snow removal services, scheduling HVAC servicing, and obtaining repair estimates for joint approval.

Section 2.3 - Joint Maintenance Fund. The Financial Administrator shall set up a dedicated joint savings account for property maintenance. Each partner will contribute a monthly amount proportional to their ownership share (totaling at least $200/month combined) to cover routine maintenance, shared repairs, and emergency expenses.

ARTICLE III: RENT APPRAISAL & EQUALIZATION MATH

Section 3.1 - Principle of Equalization. To ensure absolute fairness, the Co-owners agree that since the Main House is larger and holds higher market rental value than the ADU, this value difference must be balanced. The Co-owners shall offset this difference using the Rent Equalization Formula.

Section 3.2 - Rental Valuations. Upon occupancy, and on each 12-month anniversary thereafter, the Co-owners shall obtain a professional estimate of the fair market rental value for both the Main House and the ADU from an independent local real estate professional or appraiser.

Section 3.3 - Equalization Payment Calculation. The Rent Equalization payment shall be calculated by the Financial Administrator as follows:

Equalization Rent Formula:
Let RMain be the appraised rental value of the Main House.
Let RADU be the appraised rental value of the ADU.
Let SA and SB be the ownership splits of Party A and Party B respectively (expressed as decimals).

Rent Adjustment Payment = (RMain + RADU) × SADU_Occupant - RADU
(The occupant of the Main House shall pay the occupant of the ADU this calculated monthly difference).

Section 3.4 - Monthly Statement Integration. The calculated Rent Equalization adjustment will be applied to the monthly mortgage allocation. The partner residing in the Main House will pay their base share plus the adjustment, while the partner in the ADU will pay their base share minus the adjustment. This ensures the net out-of-pocket costs accurately reflect the value each party receives relative to their ownership share.

ARTICLE IV: EXIT PLANS, BUYOUTS & EMERGENCY BOUNDARIES

Section 4.1 - Five-Year Partnership Term. The Co-owners agree to a target partnership term of five (5) years. At the five-year mark, either partner may initiate the Valuation Buyout Protocol to dissolve the partnership, enabling each party to realize their accumulated equity and appreciation.

Section 4.2 - Valuation Buyout Protocol. To ensure a fair valuation during a buyout:

  1. The partner initiating the buyout will propose a single total market valuation for the Property (the "Proposed Value").
  2. The other partner will have forty-five (45) days to choose one of two options:
    • BUY the initiating partner's share based on the Proposed Value, or
    • SELL their own share to the initiating partner based on the Proposed Value.

Because the initiating partner does not know which option the other will choose, they are naturally incentivized to propose a highly accurate, fair market value.

Section 4.3 - Refinancing and Equity Payout. The partner buying out the property must refinance the mortgage to remove the departing partner from the deed and loan liability, paying out their equity in cash. The feasibility of this buyout is subject to the new loan meeting standard lender requirements, typically targeting a maximum 90% Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio.

Section 4.4 - Default and Emergency Protections. To safeguard the property against foreclosure, if a Co-owner fails to make their monthly payment six (6) times within a 12-month period, they will be considered in default. In the event of a default: (a) the defaulting partner must vacate their unit within thirty (30) days, (b) the buyout process will be immediately triggered, and (c) the non-defaulting partner will have the right to purchase the defaulting partner's equity, subject to a 15% reduction to cover emergency transition and refinancing costs.

ARTICLE V: EXECUTIVE MUTUAL COVENANTS & HOUSEHOLD RULES

Section 5.1 - Parking Allocation. Driveway and street parking will be divided equally. Each unit is allocated two (2) designated parking spaces. Neither partner may park commercial vehicles, trailers, or recreational vehicles in shared driveway areas without the other partner's prior written consent.

Section 5.2 - Quiet Hours. To respect each other's peace and privacy, quiet hours will be observed from 10:00 PM to 7:00 AM on weekdays, and from 11:00 PM to 8:00 AM on weekends. Loud music, power tools, or other disruptive noise is prohibited during these hours.

Section 5.3 - Pets. Co-owners may keep household pets in compliance with local city codes. Pet owners are responsible for cleaning up waste immediately in any shared outdoor areas and ensuring pets do not enter the other unit's private residence.
